A.P.j Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man" of India, was a renowned scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam's life is a testament to the power of vision and perseverance. His early years, marked by modest means, were a backdrop to his remarkable journey into the realm of science and leadership. Kalam's academic prowess led him to the Madras Institute of Technology, where he specialized in aeronautical engineering. His career in aerospace engineering began at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle, the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV), which successfully placed the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980. This achievement was a significant milestone for India's space program. Kalam's contributions were not limited to space technology; he was instrumental in advancing India's missile capabilities. His work on the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles earned him widespread acclaim and solidified his reputation as a key figure in the country's defense sector. His leadership and vision during the nuclear tests in 1998 further underscored his role in strengthening India's strategic capabilities. In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, a position he held until 2007. His presidency was characterized by his approachable nature and deep commitment to the nation's youth. He used his platform to advocate for educational reform and inspire young people to dream big and strive for excellence. His presidency earned him the affectionate title of the "People's President." After his term in office, Kalam continued to engage with the public, particularly students, through lectures and educational programs. His book, "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, has inspired millions with its narrative of resilience, hard work, and the pursuit of dreams. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His death was widely mourned, but his legacy endures. He remains a symbol of scientific excellence, visionary leadership, and inspirational guidance. Through his contributions to science, education, and public service, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam has left an indelible mark on India and the world, inspiring future generations to pursue their aspirations with determination and integrity.

Biography

  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam. His full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born in Rameswaram of Madras Presidency on 15th October 1931 and died on July 27, 2015, Shillong.
  • He was born in a Tamil Muslim Family.
  • Kalam was a vegeterian. In his words “I was forced to become a vegetarian due to financial constraints, but I eventually came to enjoy it.” Today, I am a complete vegetarian”
  • He was India’s ‘first bachelor President.
  • He was very popular among children.
  • Kalam’s autobiography ‘Wings of Fire was initially published in English Language but then later published in 13 other languages.
